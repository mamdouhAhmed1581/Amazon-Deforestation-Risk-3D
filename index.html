Project Overview & GeoAI Methodology

×

PROBLEM CONTEXT

The Amazon rainforest is undergoing accelerated deforestation driven by road expansion, agricultural pressure, and frontier development.

These pressures generate fragmented spatial patterns that are difficult to detect early using manual interpretation alone.

This platform integrates AI-based deforestation prediction with spatial analysis and interactive visualization to support environmental decision-making.

Operational Objective: We developed a GeoAI platform that integrates satellite data, machine learning prediction, and spatial analysis to identify and prioritize likely future forest loss zones.

METHODOLOGY FRAMEWORK

The proposed GeoAI framework integrates satellite data, deep learning segmentation, spatial risk modeling, and interactive Spatial visualization to estimate deforestation risk across the Amazon Basin.

Key Cycle: Satellite Data -> U-Net Segmentation -> Risk Calculation -> Spatial Validation
Model Validation Workflow: Prediction -> Satellite validation -> Accuracy assessment

ANALYSIS & VALIDATION

The model achieved an AUC score of 0.82, indicating strong predictive discrimination between higher-risk and lower-risk cells. Spatial validation was performed by comparing predicted hotspots with observed loss proxies, active fire hotspots, and satellite-based validation indicators.

Risk Factors: Forest loss history (35%), road proximity (22%), hotspot density (18%), topographic vulnerability (12%), and supporting environmental variables.

STUDY AREA: RONDONIA, BRAZIL

The study focuses on the Arc of Deforestation in Rondonia, Brazil. Approximately 150,000 spatial samples were analyzed to identify high-risk zones associated with agricultural expansion and illegal logging corridors.

The operational objective is early warning: prioritize inspections, reduce field search effort, and surface the most critical clusters first.

DEFENSE QUESTIONS

1. Why did you choose the model?
The selected model is suitable for multi-factor spatial prediction, handles non-linear relationships effectively, and supports interpretable feature ranking for key deforestation drivers.

2. What does AUC = 0.82 mean?
It means the model has strong discriminative ability to separate higher-risk cells from lower-risk cells. A value of 0.82 indicates good predictive performance above random classification.

3. How was the model validated?
Validation was performed by comparing predicted high-risk locations with observed forest loss proxies, fire hotspots, and satellite-based spatial validation indicators.

4. What is the difference between heatmap and hotspot?
Heatmap shows the overall spatial intensity of predicted risk across the region, while hotspot mode isolates the most critical clusters for direct intervention and prioritization.
